Scattered in the South China Sea lies the archipelago of El Nido –a tropical paradise that’s home to some of the most incredible islands in the world. There are secret beaches, crystal clear lagoons, coral reefs and tiny islands you wish you were stranded on.

Tach island is unique, but one thing they all have in common is the awards they have won and best hotel lists they have graced since opening. We were lucky enough to spend an idyllic two nights on Miniloc Island, the most rustic of all four resorts.

Another daily ritual on Miniloc Island is the sunset cruise.

Volcanic activity and subsequent erosion have created impressive geological features. The Big Island has the third highest point among the world’s islands.

Miniloc Island’s southeast facing position means that you can’t see the sun setting directly over the ocean.

So every evening at dusk, Miniloc Island’s boats are on standby to take guests out into Bacuit Bay to watch the sun melt into the South China Sea.
